## Idea
India is facing an unprecedented wave of AI-enabled fraud that the Prime Minister has publicly branded "Digital Arrest" scams — fraudsters impersonating CBI, ED, RBI, or police on video calls, using AI-generated voices and fake uniformed backdrops to coerce citizens into hours of isolation and fraudulent "verification" payments. A 2023 McAfee survey found 47% of Indian adults have experienced, or know someone who has experienced, an AI voice-cloning or deepfake scam — nearly double the global average — with 83% of victims losing money and nearly half losing over ₹50,000. India lost ₹22,495 crore to reported cyber fraud in 2025 alone (Ministry of Home Affairs).
In May 2026, the government's own AB PM-JAY hackathon funded deepfake detection — but only for insurance back-offices auditing hospital paperwork. India's leading detector, Vastav AI, serves police investigators. Nobody protects the ordinary citizen in the moment they actually receive the call.
Kavach is that missing layer. It instantly verifies suspicious videos, voice notes, and government notices for AI manipulation or forgery, recognizes the specific Digital Arrest scam pattern and surfaces the government's own official warning in real time, flags phishing domains and fake social profiles, and — when a scam is confirmed — lets an AI agent take over engagement with the scammer to stall them and extract intelligence, while auto-drafting the citizen's cybercrime complaint. Detection alone tells you it's fake. Kavach protects you and helps you fight back.
